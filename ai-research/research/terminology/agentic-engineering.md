## Summary
Agentic engineering is the practice of designing systems where AI agents autonomously plan, execute, and complete tasks with human supervision. Coined by Andrej Karpathy, it represents the evolution from "vibe coding" to structured, professional AI-assisted development. Rather than treating LLMs as glorified autocomplete, agentic engineering emphasizes delegation of tasks to agents with explicit goals, constraints, quality criteria, and workflows. Humans act as overseers, not authors.

## Key Concepts
- **Delegation Over Generation**: Define what to accomplish; agents figure out how
- **Supervisory Role**: Humans oversee and validate agent output, not write code directly
- **Autonomous Planning**: Agents break down goals into steps and execute recursively
- **Tool & API Integration**: Agents use tools, make decisions, and adapt based on results
- **Explicit Constraints**: Define what agents can/cannot do and quality thresholds
- **Learning Loop**: Agents improve through interaction with human feedback and environment
- **Structured Over Vibes**: Professional approach with oversight vs. unstructured "vibe coding"
- **99% Orchestration**: Default workflow is "you orchestrate agents who write code" not "you write code"
