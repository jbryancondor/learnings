## Summary
Backpressure in AI systems refers to two concepts: (1) technical—when producers generate data faster than consumers process it, creating bottlenecks; (2) human—the limited feedback capacity developers have to guide agents. In 2025, intelligent backpressure systems provide automated feedback loops, freeing human feedback capacity (backpressure) for high-value corrections rather than basic fixes. This enables agents to tackle longer-horizon tasks with more confidence.

## Key Concepts
- **Technical Backpressure**: Data production exceeds consumption; queue management required
- **Human Backpressure**: Limited developer feedback capacity constrains agent improvement
- **Automated Feedback**: Systems that provide quality/correctness feedback without human review
- **Scaling Problem**: Without backpressure, agents quickly exhaust human review capacity
- **Quality Signal**: Backpressure mechanisms provide agents with correctness signals
- **Human-in-Loop**: Reserve human backpressure for high-value decisions
- **External Systems**: Proper tool integration reduces human feedback burden
- **Constraint Mechanism**: Backpressure slows agents, prevents runaway behavior
