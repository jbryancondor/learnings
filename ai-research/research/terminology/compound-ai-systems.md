## Summary
Compound AI Systems combine multiple interacting components—multiple model calls, retrievers, tools, memory layers—into unified systems that solve problems better than single models. Rather than betting on ever-larger models, compound systems use clever engineering to distribute work across specialized components. AlphaCode 2 and AlphaGeometry prove that engineering beats scale. This democratizes AI development: state-of-the-art systems are now achievable without Google-scale compute.

## Key Concepts
- **Multiple Components**: Combine reasoning models, memory, retrieval, tools
- **Task Decomposition**: Break complex problems into specialized subtasks
- **Modular Design**: Swap components; different models/tools for different roles
- **Engineering-Over-Scale**: Clever architecture beats larger models
- **Specialized Expertise**: Each component contributes unique capabilities
- **Information Flow**: Data flows through components; each adds value
- **Orchestration**: Coordinate components to achieve emergent capabilities
- **Cost Efficiency**: Specialized components cost less than single large models
