## Summary
Compounding engineering is a development philosophy where each unit of engineering work makes subsequent units easier, not harder. Traditional engineering accumulates complexity; compounding engineering accumulates knowledge. By creating learning loops that document bugs, tests, and insights, subsequent agents (AI or human) develop faster. This creates a flywheel where codebase complexity grows, but so does AI knowledge, enabling exponential productivity gains.

## Key Concepts
- **Compound Effect**: Each engineering task reduces friction for the next
- **Learning Loops**: Document problems and solutions so future agents learn
- **Knowledge Accumulation**: AI's knowledge of codebase grows with each task
- **Flywheel**: Complexity growth matched by knowledge growth = net positive acceleration
- **Sustainable Growth**: Avoids the common trap where each feature makes the next harder
- **Agent-Friendly Architecture**: Codebase designed to be understandable and modifiable by AI
- **Documentation as Capital**: Explicit capture of decisions and patterns becomes competitive advantage
