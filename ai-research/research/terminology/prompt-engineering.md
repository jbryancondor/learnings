## Summary
Prompt engineering is the process of structuring natural language inputs (prompts) to produce specified outputs from generative AI models. It involves crafting clear, unambiguous requests specific enough to get desired results. The field includes 50+ distinct techniques and represents a shift from "magic words" to systematic context optimization. In 2025, prompt engineering is recognized as a subset of the broader discipline of context engineering.

## Key Concepts
- **Prompt**: Natural language input that conditions an LLM's output
- **Prompt Design**: Crafting clear, specific instructions that minimize ambiguity
- **Technique-Driven Approaches**: Chain-of-Thought, Few-Shot, Role Prompting, Tree-of-Thought, etc.
- **Context Window Optimization**: Using available tokens to maximize instruction clarity and context
- **Iterative Refinement**: Testing, evaluating, and improving prompts based on output quality
- **Context Engineering Era**: Shift from prompt-only to full-context optimization (2025+)
- **Prompting Taxonomy**: 50+ techniques identified as of 2024

## Quotes / Evidence

## Relevance to Generative AI Engineer Role
Prompt engineering is a foundational skill, but increasingly secondary to context engineering. You'll spend less time on "clever prompts" and more on orchestrating context—managing retrieved documents, conversation history, tool definitions, specs, and output formats. Understanding prompt design principles helps, but the real leverage is in engineering complete information flows.
