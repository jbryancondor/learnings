## Summary
Temperature and Top-P are hyperparameters that control the randomness and diversity of LLM outputs during token sampling. Temperature scales the probability distribution (0.0–2.0, default 1.0), while Top-P (nucleus sampling) limits generation to the top tokens whose cumulative probability exceeds a threshold (typically 0.7–0.95). Together they enable balancing between deterministic, focused outputs and creative, diverse ones.

## Key Concepts
- **Temperature**: Controls randomness; lower (0.1–0.3) = focused/deterministic, higher (1.5–2.0) = creative/diverse
- **Top-P (Nucleus Sampling)**: Restricts tokens to those with cumulative probability > P (e.g., 0.95)
- **Softmax Scaling**: Temperature modifies logit values before softmax, flattening or sharpening probability distributions
- **Token Selection**: The model samples from the filtered/scaled probability distribution
- **Coherence vs. Variety**: Temperature controls the trade-off between coherent repetition and creative novelty
- **Provider Defaults**: Most providers default to temperature=1.0 and top_p=1.0 (all tokens considered)

## Relevance to Generative AI Engineer Role
Temperature and Top-P are your primary levers for output control. You'll tune these parameters based on the task: low temperature for factual tasks (e.g., customer support), higher for creative ones (e.g., brainstorming). In production, you'll need monitoring to ensure outputs stay within acceptable quality bounds. These settings are essential for balancing consistency with user satisfaction.
