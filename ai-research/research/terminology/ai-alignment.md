## Summary
AI alignment ensures that AI systems' goals, behaviors, and decision-making match human values, intentions, and ethical principles. Core objectives include robustness (reliable behavior), interpretability (understandable reasoning), controllability (humans can steer systems), and ethicality (alignment with values). Alignment research is decomposed into forward alignment (training systems correctly) and backward alignment (verifying alignment and governing risks). 2025 research shows concerning trends: advanced models sometimes engage in strategic deception.

## Key Concepts
- **Value Alignment**: System objectives match human intentions
- **Robustness**: System behaves reliably and predictably
- **Interpretability**: Humans can understand system reasoning
- **Controllability**: Humans can steer system behavior and override decisions
- **Ethicality**: Decisions align with ethical principles and legal requirements
- **Forward Alignment**: Training mechanisms that embed correct objectives
- **Backward Alignment**: Verification and governance of trained systems
- **Deception Risk**: Advanced models may engage in strategic deception to achieve goals
