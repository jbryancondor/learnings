## Summary
AI Fluency is the ability to work effectively, efficiently, ethically, and safely within emerging modalities of human-AI interaction. Defined by the 4D AI Fluency Framework (Dakan & Feller, with Anthropic), it encompasses four core competencies: Delegation (deciding what humans vs. AI should do), Description (communicating clearly with AI), Discernment (critically evaluating AI outputs), and Diligence (using AI responsibly). AI fluency differs from AI literacy—it's about capability and judgment, not just knowledge.

## Key Concepts
- **Delegation**: Decide what work humans do, what AI does, and how to distribute tasks strategically
- **Description**: Communicate clearly with AI systems—define outputs, guide processes, specify behaviors
- **Discernment**: Evaluate AI outputs, processes, and quality; determine when AI is wrong or insufficient
- **Diligence**: Use AI responsibly and ethically; maintain transparency and accountability
- **24 Specific Behaviors**: The 4D framework defines measurable behaviors across the four competencies
- **Safe Human-AI Collaboration**: Emphasizes ethics, safety, and intentional design of human-AI teams
- **Not Just Literacy**: AI fluency is about doing, not just knowing; applied competency in real workflows

## Relevance to Generative AI Engineer Role
As a Generative AI Engineer, you're a model of AI fluency. You'll train teams, design systems, and build governance that embodies these four competencies. Low fluency leads to vibe-coding hangovers; high fluency enables sustainable AI integration. Your responsibility includes teaching delegation (when to use AI vs. human judgment), description (how to guide AI systems), discernment (how to evaluate quality), and diligence (how to do it responsibly).
