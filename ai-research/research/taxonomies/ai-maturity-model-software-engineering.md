## Summary

The AI-MM SET (AI Maturity Model for Software Engineering Teams) is an **open-source framework** specifically designed for engineering teams. It maps 5 maturity levels across 6 dimensions and includes role-based expectations from junior to distinguished engineer. Uniquely practical for team assessment and individual growth planning.

## Key Concepts

### 5 Maturity Levels

| Level | Name | Description |
|-------|------|-------------|
| 1 | **Exploratory** | AI usage unstructured, variable. No shared practices or oversight. |
| 2 | **Applied** | AI tools introduced via pilots or individual initiative. Organic practices emerging. |
| 3 | **Standardized** | Consistent AI usage across teams. Peer review incorporates AI outputs. Governance enforced. |
| 4 | **Strategic** | AI embedded across entire SDLC. AI-first workflow design. Proactive governance. |
| 5 | **Transformational** | Engineers and AI as collaborative partners. Roles evolve to supervise/orchestrate AI. Continuous innovation. |

### 6 Core Dimensions

1. **AI Literacy & Competency** — From basic awareness to industry-leading expertise
2. **Workflow & SDLC Integration** — From fragmented usage to AI-native development
3. **Tooling Integration** — From disconnected tools to intelligent adaptive platforms
4. **Trust, Safety & Governance** — From absent policies to institutionalized responsible AI
5. **AI-Augmented Collaboration** — From isolated usage to human-AI co-creation
6. **Business Impact & Innovation** — From unmeasured gains to transformational capabilities

### Role-Based Expectations

| Role | Focus |
|------|-------|
| Junior | Build foundational fluency through experimentation |
| Mid-Level | Apply AI to accelerate delivery, influence team workflows |
| Senior | Lead by example, mentor peers in responsible adoption |
| Staff | Drive integration into team practices and architecture |
| Principal | Align adoption with business goals, steer platform innovation |
| Distinguished | Set organizational direction, influence industry standards |

## Relevance to Generative AI Engineer Role

This is the most directly applicable framework for a software engineer transitioning to Gen AI engineering. The role-based expectations provide a concrete rubric: "What should I be doing at my level?" The 6-dimension model also helps identify blind spots — you might be Level 4 in tooling but Level 2 in governance. Use this alongside Eledath's 8 Levels for complementary individual + team assessment.
