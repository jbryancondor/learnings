## Summary

Gartner's "Emerging Tech: Agentic AI Maturity Roadmap" (August 2025) defines **5 maturity stages** progressing from basic task automation to expert autonomous systems. This is the most widely cited analyst framework. The actual report is gated, but key details are available through Gartner press releases, partner summaries, and the broader Gartner AI maturity toolkit.

Gartner's broader AI maturity model uses 5 levels: Awareness → Active → Operational → Systemic → Transformational.

## Key Concepts

### 5 Agentic AI Maturity Stages (Gartner)

The report defines progression from:
1. **Basic task automation** — Simple, rules-based agents
2. **Task-specific agents** — Single-domain autonomous operation
3. **Coordinated agents** — Multi-agent interaction within bounded domains
4. **Advanced autonomous systems** — Cross-domain orchestration with adaptive learning
5. **Expert autonomous systems** — Mission-critical, self-improving agent ecosystems

### Broader Gartner AI Maturity Model

| Level | Name | Focus |
|-------|------|-------|
| 1 | Awareness | Understanding AI potential |
| 2 | Active | Isolated experiments and pilots |
| 3 | Operational | Standardized, governed AI in production |
| 4 | Systemic | Enterprise-wide integration |
| 5 | Transformational | AI-driven business model innovation |

### Key Predictions

- 40% of enterprise apps will feature task-specific AI agents by 2026 (up from <5% in 2025)
- Over 40% of agentic AI projects will be canceled by end of 2027 (governance/ROI failures)
- 60% of brands will use agentic AI for one-to-one interactions by 2028
- 80% of common customer service issues resolved autonomously by 2029

### Strategic Recommendations

- Plan R&D investments around capability development priorities at each stage
- Product leaders must design roadmaps for upcoming advanced and expert AI agents
- Prepare for continuous disruption — agentic AI evolves from experimental to mission-critical
