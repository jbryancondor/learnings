## Summary

Forrester's 2026 AI predictions focus on the **hype-to-value transition** — "AI will inevitably lose its sheen, trading its tiara for a hard hat." Key data points: only 15% of AI decision-makers report EBITDA lift, enterprises will delay 25% of AI spend to 2027, and 50% of organizations are piloting agentic AI while only 24% have it in production.

Not a formal maturity model, but provides critical empirical context for where enterprises actually sit on the maturity spectrum.

## Key Concepts

### The Reality Check

- Only **15%** of AI decision-makers reported EBITDA lift in the past 12 months
- Fewer than **1/3** can tie AI value to P&L changes
- Enterprises will delay **25% of planned AI spend** to 2027
- **30%** of large enterprises will mandate AI training (governance driver)

### Agentic AI Adoption Status (Q4 2025)

| Status | Percentage |
|--------|-----------|
| Piloting agentic AI | 50% |
| In production | 24% |
| Not started | 26% |

### Key Predictions

1. AI shifts from "hype to hard hat work" — ROI discipline replaces vendor hyperbole
2. Governance and training become core maturity drivers
3. 30% of large enterprises will mandate AI training to reduce risk
4. Value realization will define winners vs. perpetual experimenters

### Implications for Maturity Models

Forrester's data suggests most organizations are at:
- Eledath Level 2–3 (Agent IDE / Context Engineering)
- Microsoft L100–L200 (Initial / Repeatable)
- Nexocode Crawl–Walk stage
- Gartner Awareness–Active stage

The 25% spend deferral signals that many organizations are **retreating** from premature advancement, not progressing forward.
